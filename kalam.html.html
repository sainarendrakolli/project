A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931-2015

missile man of india

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, known as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Growing up in a humble background, he overcame numerous challenges to become one of India's most revered scientists and statesmen. Kalam's early education was in Rameswaram, and he later graduated in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology. His brilliance in the field of aerospace engineering led to his pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs.

Dr. Kalam's illustrious career at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) laid the foundation for his reputation as the architect of India's space technology and defense capabilities. His significant contributions include the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and various ballistic missile systems. In 2002, Abdul Kalam became the 11th President of India, serving in this esteemed role until 2007. During his presidency, he continued to emphasize the importance of education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, aiming to inspire the youth to pursue knowledge and innovation.

Beyond his scientific achievements and political tenure, Abdul Kalam was renowned for his humility, integrity, and dedication to the welfare of the people. After leaving the presidency, he remained actively involved in educational initiatives and continued to inspire students across the country through lectures and writings. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy lives on as an inspirational figure, symbolizing the power of education, determination, and service to humanity in the pursuit of national development.
